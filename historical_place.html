Taj Mahal

About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the most famous historical monuments in the world. It is a beautiful white marble mausoleum built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

It is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, on the banks of the Yamuna River. The construction of the Taj Mahal started in 1632 and was completed in 1653 during the Mughal period.

The Taj Mahal is historically important because it represents the rich cultural heritage of India and is a symbol of love and devotion. It is also recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The monument attracts millions of tourists every year due to its stunning architecture, symmetry, and beautiful gardens surrounding it.

Interesting Facts

  • The Taj Mahal changes color during different times of the day.
  • It took around 20 years to complete the construction.
  • More than 20,000 workers were involved in building it.
  • It is one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
